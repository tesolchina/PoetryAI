## Poetry Writing Tips

### For Beginners (No Experience Required!)

**Don't worry if you've never written poetry before!** Our study is designed for all levels. Here are some tips:

1. **Start Simple:**
   - Pick a topic you care about
   - Use everyday language
   - Don't worry about rhyming

2. **Use Your Senses:**
   - What do you see, hear, smell, taste, touch?
   - Sensory details make poetry vivid
   - Example: Instead of "happy," describe "sunshine warming my face"

3. **Show, Don't Tell:**
   - Use images and examples
   - Let readers experience your feelings
   - Example: Instead of "I was sad," write "Rain clouds gathered in my chest"

4. **Play with Words:**
   - Experiment with different phrasings
   - Try metaphors and comparisons
   - Read your lines aloud to hear the rhythm

5. **Be Authentic:**
   - Write about what matters to you
   - Your unique perspective is valuable
   - There's no "wrong" way to write poetry

### Possible Topics to Explore

- **Personal experiences:** memories, dreams, feelings
- **Nature:** seasons, weather, landscapes, animals
- **Relationships:** friendship, family, love
- **Places:** your hometown, HKBU campus, favorite spots
- **Abstract concepts:** time, change, hope, fear
- **Cultural themes:** traditions, identity, language
- **Current events:** social issues, technology, education
