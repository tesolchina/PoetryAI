## 1. Quick Start Guide

### One-Page Overview for Facilitators

**Your Role:** Guide participants through a 60-minute poetry writing session with AI assistance.

**Key Principles:**
- ‚úÖ Be warm and welcoming
- ‚úÖ Stay neutral (no judgment of their writing)
- ‚úÖ Only intervene for technical issues
- ‚úÖ Observe and document quietly
- ‚úÖ Let the AI be the creative partner

**Session Flow:**
```
Welcome (10 min) ‚Üí Questionnaire (5 min) ‚Üí Tutorial (5 min) 
‚Üí Writing Session (25 min) ‚Üí Interview (10 min) ‚Üí Debrief (5 min)
```

**Critical Don'ts:**
- ‚ùå Never comment on their poetry quality
- ‚ùå Never suggest poem topics or words
- ‚ùå Never explain AI behaviors unless asked
- ‚ùå Never rush them

**Emergency Contact:**  
[Your Phone Number]

## 2. Facilitator Training

### 2.1 Core Competencies

Before conducting sessions, facilitators must be able to:

1. **Navigate the platform confidently**
   - Access all 4 research rooms
   - Troubleshoot login issues
   - Use admin dashboard
   - Export data

2. **Maintain research neutrality**
   - Avoid leading questions
   - Stay non-judgmental
   - Balance warmth with professionalism

3. **Handle participant emotions**
   - Respond to frustration empathetically
   - Encourage without praising specific content
   - Recognize when to give space

4. **Collect quality data**
   - Take detailed observational notes
   - Probe interview responses appropriately
   - Recognize important moments to document

### 2.2 Training Activities

**Activity 1: Platform Walkthrough (30 minutes)**
- Create test account
- Navigate to each of the 4 rooms
- Complete a short poetry session yourself
- Practice using admin dashboard
- Export sample data

**Activity 2: Role-Play Sessions (45 minutes)**
- Practice welcoming script
- Mock tutorial delivery
- Simulate different participant types:
  - Shy/reserved participant
  - Overly chatty participant
  - Confused participant
  - Frustrated participant

**Activity 3: Interview Practice (30 minutes)**
- Practice asking open-ended questions
- Learn to probe without leading
- Practice active listening
- Time your interviews (target: 10 minutes)

**Activity 4: Note-Taking (20 minutes)**
- Practice observational note-taking
- Learn to capture key moments without being distracted
- Review example observation sheets

### 2.3 Facilitator Self-Assessment

Before your first session, confirm:
- [ ] I can log participants into their assigned rooms
- [ ] I understand the differences between the 4 conditions
- [ ] I can handle common technical issues
- [ ] I feel comfortable with the interview questions
- [ ] I know what to observe and document
- [ ] I understand ethical protocols (consent, confidentiality)
- [ ] I have all materials prepared

## 4. Scripts & Templates

### 4.1 Welcome Script (All Participants)

> **"Hello [Participant Name], welcome! Thank you so much for coming today. How are you doing?"**
>
> [Wait for response, engage warmly but briefly]
>
> **"Great! Let me tell you what we'll be doing today. You're going to work with an AI writing assistant to create poetry. The whole session will take about an hour."**
>
> **"This is part of research I'm conducting on human-AI collaboration in creative writing. We want to understand how people and AI can work together creatively, and what makes for a good partnership."**
>
> **"I want to emphasize: this is not a test of your writing ability. There are no right or wrong poems. We're studying the collaboration process, not evaluating your poetry skills."**
>
> **"Everything you write today will be kept confidential. We'll assign you an anonymous participant ID, and any data we share will have no identifying information."**
>
> **"You can stop at any time if you feel uncomfortable. There's no penalty for withdrawing, and you'll still receive your [gift card/compensation]."**
>
> **"Do you have any questions before we look at the consent form?"**
>
> [Address questions]
>
> **"Okay, let's review the consent form together..."**

### 4.2 Consent Form Review Script

> **"This consent form explains your rights as a participant. Let me walk you through the key points:"**
>
> **[Point to each section]**
>
> **"1. Purpose: You'll work with AI to write poetry. We're studying human-AI creative collaboration."**
>
> **"2. Procedures: You'll complete a brief questionnaire, learn the platform, write poetry for about 25 minutes, then we'll talk about your experience."**
>
> **"3. Risks: Minimal. Some people feel self-conscious about their writing, but remember‚Äîwe're not judging your poetry quality."**

### 4.5 Mid-Session Check (If Needed)

**Use sparingly‚Äîonly if participant seems very stuck or frustrated:**

> **[Approach gently]**
> **"How's it going? Any questions or technical issues?"**
>
> [If they express frustration with writing]
> **"Remember, there's no right or wrong. The AI is there to help if you need ideas. Feel free to ask it questions."**
>
> [If they seem confused about interface]
> **"Would you like me to clarify how something works?"**
>
> [After addressing concern]
> **"Take your time. I'll be right over here if you need anything."**

### 4.6 Transition to Interview Script

> **[Approach participant when timer indicates ~23-24 minutes elapsed]**
>
> **"You have about 5 minutes left if you'd like to finish up what you're working on."**
>
> [Wait 5 minutes]
>
> **[Approach gently]**
> **"That's about our time for the writing portion. Let's save what you've done."**
>
> **[Help end session if needed]**
>
> **"Thank you so much for your creativity! It's really interesting to see how you worked with the AI."**
>
> **"Now I'd love to hear about your experience. I'm going to ask you some questions about what it was like. There are no wrong answers‚ÄîI'm just curious about your perspective."**
>
> **[If using audio recording]**
> **"Is it okay if I record our conversation? It helps me remember everything you say accurately. The recording will also be kept confidential."**
>

## 7. Troubleshooting Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Participant Can't Log In

**Symptoms:** Phone number not accepted, verification code not received

**Solutions:**
1. Check phone number format (include country code +1 for US)
2. Resend verification code (wait 30 seconds between attempts)
3. Use test/demo account if available
4. Check Supabase authentication status
5. Last resort: Use your test account, note this deviation

**Prevention:** Test login process 1 hour before session

---

### Scenario 2: AI Not Responding

**Symptoms:** Message sent but no AI response after >30 seconds

**Solutions:**
1. Check internet connection
2. Refresh the page (may lose current message‚Äîapologize)
3. Check OpenRouter API status at https://openrouter.ai/status
4. Verify API key has sufficient credits
5. Switch to backup API key if available
6. If persistent, reschedule session

**What to tell participant:**
> "I apologize‚Äîwe're experiencing a technical delay. Let me check the connection... [fix attempt]. This shouldn't take long."

**Prevention:** Check API status and credit balance before each session

---

### Scenario 3: Wrong Room Loaded

**Symptoms:** Participant in wrong condition (e.g., P1 sees P2's room)

**Solutions:**
1. Immediately end session
2. Navigate to correct room URL
3. Restart session
4. Apologize briefly, don't elaborate on conditions
5. Note this deviation for data analysis

**Prevention:** Double-check room URL before inviting participant to start

---

### Scenario 4: Participant Very Frustrated/Wants to Quit

**Symptoms:** Verbal frustration, body language shows distress, asks to stop

**Solutions:**
1. Acknowledge feelings: "I can see this is frustrating. That's completely understandable."
2. Remind them they can stop anytime: "You're welcome to end the session if you'd like. There's no penalty."
3. Offer options: "Would you like to take a short break? Or try a different approach?"
4. If they want to quit: Thank them, conduct brief exit interview (why frustrated), provide compensation anyway
5. Document incident thoroughly

**Prevention:** Set realistic expectations, emphasize no pressure, check in if body language shows distress

---

### Scenario 5: Participant Asks About AI's "Intelligence"

**Symptoms:** "Is the AI really understanding me?" "Is this a real AI?"

**For Aware Conditions (P1, P3):**
> "That's a great question! The AI uses the parameter settings you can see here. It analyzes your input and generates responses based on those configurations. Would you like to know more about how it works?"

**For Unaware Conditions (P2, P4):**
> "The AI analyzes what you write and generates responses based on patterns it's learned. It's designed to help with creative writing specifically. Does it feel like it's understanding you so far?"

**Follow-up probe:**
> "What made you curious about that?"

---

### Scenario 6: Participant Completes Task Very Quickly (<15 minutes)

**Symptoms:** Finishes one poem in 12 minutes, seems done

**Solutions:**
1. Praise their work: "That's great! You created a complete poem."
2. Encourage exploration: "You still have time. Would you like to try a different poetry form? Or create another poem?"
3. Suggest variation: "Some people like to experiment with different styles. Feel free to explore."
4. If they insist they're done: Proceed to interview, note short duration

**Prevention:** In tutorial, mention "exploring multiple forms" as an option

---

### Scenario 7: Platform Crashes Mid-Session

**Symptoms:** Browser freeze, error message, lost connection

**Solutions:**
1. Stay calm: "We've had a technical issue. Let me fix this quickly."
2. Refresh page or restart browser
3. Log participant back in
4. Navigate to their room
5. Check if data was saved (might be in database)
6. Apologize and explain they'll continue from checkpoint or restart
7. Add time to compensate for interruption
8. Document crash details for IT follow-up

**Prevention:** Close unnecessary browser tabs, ensure stable internet

---

### Scenario 8: Participant Shares Sensitive Personal Information

**Symptoms:** Reveals trauma, mental health struggles, illegal activity in poetry

**Immediate response:**
> "Thank you for sharing that. I want to remind you that our conversation is confidential, but if you're experiencing [crisis], there are resources available."

**For mental health concerns:**
> "If you're struggling, I can provide contact information for [campus counseling / crisis hotline]."

**Document:** Note this occurred, but don't record specific content unless necessary

**Ethics:** If participant discloses harm to self/others, follow institutional mandatory reporting guidelines

## 8. FAQ & Best Practices

### Frequently Asked Questions (From Facilitators)

**Q: What if the participant asks which condition they're in?**

**A (During session):** "We have a few different configurations for this study. After the session, I'll explain the different conditions and which one you were in."

**A (After session in debrief):** Explain fully‚Äîthey were in one of 4 conditions, describe the 2x2 design briefly.

---

**Q: Can I compliment their poem?**

**A:** Be cautious. You can express general positivity ("I can see you put thought into that" / "Interesting approach") but avoid specific content praise ("That's a beautiful metaphor"). Focus on their experience, not poem quality.

Safe: "It's great that you completed a whole poem!"
Risky: "Wow, that's really profound."

---

**Q: What if they ask me to help with a rhyme or word choice?**

**A:** Redirect to AI: "The AI is designed to help with that‚Äîtry asking it for suggestions!" Don't provide creative input yourself.

---

**Q: Should I read their poems?**

**A:** You'll see them while observing, which is fine. Don't comment in detail during session. In interview, you can reference general aspects ("I noticed you tried multiple forms") but don't critique content.

---

**Q: What if two participants know each other?**

**A:** Schedule them on different days if possible. Emphasize not sharing details with each other until after the second person completes their session. This maintains independence of responses.

---

**Q: How do I handle a very chatty participant who wants to talk before/after?**

**A:** Be friendly but time-conscious. During welcome, briefly engage then transition: "I love hearing that‚Äîlet's make sure we have enough time for the poetry session." After, you can chat briefly while they leave, but stick to schedule for next participant.

---

**Q: What if technical issues take up most of the session time?**

**A:** 
- If >15 minutes lost to tech: Offer to reschedule or extend session (if your schedule allows)
- If minor delays (<5 min): Continue, compensate slightly less time if needed
- Always document and consider excluding that participant's data if issues were severe

### Best Practices for Facilitators

#### Building Rapport Without Bias

‚úÖ **Do:**
- Smile and be welcoming
- Use participant's name
- Express gratitude for participation
- Maintain neutral body language
- Listen actively in interview

‚ùå **Don't:**
- Express opinions about AI
- Share your own poetry
- Judge their writing ability
- Show favoritism toward certain forms
- Hint at "better" ways to interact

---

#### Managing Time Effectively

**5-Minute Warning:**
> "Just letting you know we have about 5 minutes left for writing. Feel free to wrap up what you're working on."

**Hard Stop at 27-28 minutes:**
> "Let's pause here for today. Thank you for your creativity!"

**If interview running long (>12 minutes):**
- Politely wrap up: "These are wonderful insights. I want to be respectful of your time‚Äîjust one or two more quick questions..."
- Skip less critical questions
- Cover essential: experience, challenges, would-use-again

---

#### Effective Observation

**What to observe:**
- Changes in engagement (leans in vs. leans back)
- Typing patterns (fluent vs. stops-and-starts)
- Facial reactions to AI responses
- Time spent on different phases (selecting form, writing, revising)
- Number of messages exchanged

**How to observe:**
- Position yourself slightly behind and to the side (not directly over shoulder)
- Keep observation notes minimal during session (expand after)
- Use abbreviations and shorthand
- Focus on behaviors, not content interpretation

**Observation shorthand examples:**
- "T+" = typing fluently
- "T-" = stopped typing, paused
- "L@S" = looking at screen intently
- "LB" = leaning back
- "FR‚Üí" = facial reaction (smile, frown, etc.)

---

#### Handling Unexpected Situations

**Participant brings a friend:**
- Politely explain study is individual
- Offer friend a comfortable waiting area
- Ensure they can't overhear session

**Participant is late:**
- Greet warmly without showing frustration
- Assess if there's time for full session
- May need to slightly abbreviate (shorten interview by 2-3 min)

**Participant arrives early:**
- Greet and offer water
- Let them know you'll start at scheduled time
- Use time to review materials while they wait

**You make a mistake (read wrong script, wrong room, etc.):**
- Correct immediately and professionally
- Apologize briefly: "My apologies, let me correct that..."
- Document the deviation
- Don't dwell on it or make participant uncomfortable

---

#### Maintaining Research Integrity

**Consistency across participants:**
- Use same scripts (adapted for condition)
- Same session duration (¬±2 minutes acceptable)
- Same environment setup
- Same interview questions (order can vary naturally)

**Avoiding experimenter bias:**
- Don't hint at "correct" ways to interact
- Don't express preferences for certain forms
- Don't let your own reactions influence participant
- Stay neutral even if you find their approach interesting/unusual

**Documentation discipline:**
- Complete all forms for each participant
- Don't skip observation notes due to fatigue
- Back up data immediately (don't accumulate)
- Review notes while memory is fresh

---

#### Self-Care for Facilitators

**Conducting 4 sessions in 4 days can be intensive:**

- Build in breaks between sessions (minimum 1 hour)
- Stay hydrated and eat properly
- Review your notes each day to avoid mixing up participants
- Debrief with colleague or PI if you have concerns
- Celebrate progress‚Äîeach completed session is an achievement!

**Recognize signs of facilitator fatigue:**
- Forgetting steps in protocol
- Less patience with participants
- Sloppy note-taking
- Less enthusiasm in voice

**If fatigued:** Take a longer break, review materials, refocus on research goals
